Breastfeeding Awareness

1. Breastfeeding Education

Importance of Breastfeeding:

  • Breast milk is the ideal nutrition for infants. It contains all nutrients in correct proportions for the first 6 months.
  • It provides antibodies that help babies fight viruses and bacteria.
  • Reduces risk of diarrhea, pneumonia, ear infections in infants.
  • Promotes emotional bonding between mother and baby.
  • Helps in uterine contraction and reduces postpartum bleeding.
  • Long-term benefits: Reduces risk of obesity, diabetes, and breast cancer in mothers.

Before Breastfeeding (During Pregnancy):

  • Eat a Balanced Diet: Rice, wheat, dal, milk, eggs, vegetables, fruits, nuts
  • Eat Protein-Rich Foods: Milk, curd, dal, eggs, chicken, fish, paneer
  • Iron-Rich Foods: Greens, dates, jaggery, dry fruits
  • Drink lemon juice to help absorb iron

During Breastfeeding (Diet):

  • More Protein: Milk, dal, egg, sprouts, chicken

  • Avoid or Limit: Too much tea/coffee, alcohol, spicy or junk food

Benefits of Breastfeeding

For the Baby:

  • Increased bonding
  • Improved cognitive and motor development
  • Improved growth and nutritional status
  • Boosts immunity
  • Improves digestion
  • Healthy weight gain
  • Lower risk of chronic diseases (diabetes, heart disease, asthma)

For the Mother:

  • Faster maternal recovery
  • Less postpartum depression
  • Lower risk of ovarian and breast cancer
  • Cost-effective
  • Promotes bonding
  • Faster weight loss
  • Less likely to become pregnant in early months

Care of Breast â€“ Before & After Feeding

Before Feeding:

  • Wash hands with soap and water
  • Check nipples for cracks or soreness
  • Wipe breast gently with clean cloth (no need to wash every time)
  • Sit comfortably and support the breast

After Feeding:

  • Let a few drops of breast milk dry on the nipple
  • Keep breast and nipple dry
  • Wear clean, soft cotton bra
  • Change breast pads if they are wet
  • Avoid tight clothing

When to Express Milk:

  • Baby is not able to suck properly
  • Mother is away from the baby (e.g., work)
  • To relieve breast engorgement (fullness)

Storage of Expressed Milk:

  • Refrigerator: up to 4 days
  • Freezer: up to 6 months

Milk Bank

A milk bank is a place where breast milk donated by healthy mothers is collected, tested, stored, and given to babies who need it.

Who needs donated milk?

  • Premature babies
  • Sick or weak babies
  • Babies whose mothers cannot produce milk

Steps in Milk Banking:

  1. Healthy mothers donate extra milk
  2. Milk is tested and safely stored
  3. Given to babies in hospitals

Is it safe?

Yes! All milk is tested, pasteurized (gently heated), and handled safely.

Who can donate?

  • Healthy mothers
  • Not taking harmful medicines
  • Willing to follow clean and safe methods
